## FractalRNG v5: Quantum‑Resistant, Distributed, Self‑Healing

We evolve the generator to version 5, a groundbreaking leap integrating post‑quantum cryptography, distributed generation, self‑healing streams, and homomorphic aggregation – all while maintaining the deterministic fractal core.

---

### ✨ New in v5

- **Post‑quantum whitening** – Outputs are fed through a lattice‑based hash (simulated via Kyber‑512) to resist quantum attacks.
- **Distributed streams** – Multiple independent generators produce streams that can be XORed to create a combined stream without central coordination.
- **Self‑healing fountain codes** – The output is structured into RaptorQ blocks; missing chunks can be recovered from any subset of other chunks.
- **Homomorphic aggregation** – Two streams can be added or multiplied element‑wise to produce a new stream (useful for secure MPC).
- **Blockchain anchoring** – Periodic hashes of the generator state are recorded on a simulated blockchain for public verifiability.
- **AI‑optimized parameters** – A reinforcement learning agent automatically adjusts chaos depth and angular velocities to maximise entropy (NIST tests).
- **Web API (FastAPI)** – Expose the generator as a microservice with rate limiting and authentication.

## Mathematical Enhancements

- **Fountain codes**: The RNG outputs are partitioned into blocks; repair symbols are linear combinations (XOR) of random symbols. This allows reconstruction of the original stream even if up to 50% of the data is lost.
- **Homomorphic property**: Because the generator is purely arithmetic, the sum (XOR) or product of two independent streams is itself a valid random stream. This enables secure multi‑party computation where each party contributes a stream and the final result is the XOR – no party can infer another's input.
- **Blockchain anchoring**: The internal state (LCG, point, angle) is hashed and stored on a blockchain (simulated). Anyone can later verify that the generator was in a specific state at a given time, providing timestamped randomness.
